Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is the difference between "var", "let", and "const"?
"var" is function-scoped, while "let" and "const" are block-scoped. "let" can be reassigned, but "const" is immutable.
2. What is the difference between "map()", "forEach()", and "filter()"?
"map()" creates a new array with transformed data, "forEach()" performs an action for each array element without returning anything, and "filter()" creates a new array with elements that meet a condition.
3. What are arrow functions, and how are they different from regular functions?
Arrow functions have a concise syntax, inherit "this" from their parent scope, and cannot be used as constructors, unlike regular functions.
4. How do JavaScript Promises work?
Promises represent asynchronous operations with states: pending, resolved, or rejected. They simplify chaining and error handling using ".then()" and ".catch()".
5. What are closures in JavaScript?
Closures are functions that retain access to variables from their outer scope, even after the outer function has finished execution.
